AI-Assisted Early Sepsis Detection in Emergency Department Triage: A Prospective Clinical Study

What does it cover? This paper evaluates a machine learning triage model designed to detect high-risk sepsis patients during the first 30 minutes of Emergency Department assessment.

Summary: The study analyzes vital signs, initial laboratory indicators, and triage notes to predict sepsis progression and ICU admission risk. Results demonstrate improved early recognition, faster antibiotic initiation, and better protocol adherence when compared with standard triage workflows.

Emergency Medicine April 12, 2026

Point-of-Care Ultrasound in Undifferentiated Shock: Impact on Early Decision-Making in the ED

What does it cover? This study examines the role of bedside ultrasound protocols in identifying shock etiology during initial emergency department resuscitation.

Summary: Investigators compared standard clinical assessment with focused ultrasound-supported assessment. Findings show shorter diagnostic time, more targeted fluid and vasopressor use, and improved early disposition planning for critically ill patients.
